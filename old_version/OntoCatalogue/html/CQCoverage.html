CQCoverage: Closure-based fraction of gold competency questions covered by the predicted ontology

This metric measures how completely a predicted ontology answers the gold-standard competency questions (CQs). It is computed in the hierarchy step (the fifth and final evaluation stage) and strictly inherits all true positives from the preceding axiom step — a gold axiom can only be upgraded to TP here, never demoted.

The strict axiom step requires gold and predicted axioms to match exactly. The closure step adds the missing reasoning: it expands subClassOf / subPropertyOf transitively in the prediction and rescues gold axioms that hold under that closure.

Calculation Process:

  1. Decompose each gold axiom into atomic hierarchy pairs. SubClassOf / SubPropertyOf produce one pair (sub, sup). Complex axioms expand to several — for example EquivalentClasses(A, B ⊓ C ⊓ D) produces three pairs (A ⊑ B), (A ⊑ C), (A ⊑ D).
  2. Compute the predicted ontology's transitive closure over the asserted subclass and subproperty edges.
  3. Match each atomic pair against the closure (or against the asserted edges directly).
  4. Rescue rule (all-or-nothing). A gold axiom is rescued only if every one of its atomic pairs is matched. Partial coverage counts as a failure, because the semantics of the gold axiom are incomplete.
  5. Mark TP if the gold axiom was already TP at the strict axiom step or is fully rescued here.
  6. Aggregate per CQ. A CQ is fully covered when every gold axiom tagged with it is TP. The CQ-level rate is n_tp / n_axioms. Dataset-level metrics: strict fully-covered ratio, at-least-one-match ratio, and mean per-CQ rate.

Example:

Gold axiom for CQ7: EquivalentClasses(SeafoodWine, WhiteWine ⊓ DryWine ⊓ pairsWith.Seafood) → 3 atomic pairs.

  • The prediction asserts SeafoodWine ⊑ WhiteWine directly → matched.
  • The prediction asserts SeafoodWine ⊑ DryWhiteWine and DryWhiteWine ⊑ DryWine, so SeafoodWine ⊑ DryWine is in the closure → matched.
  • The prediction does not mention pairsWith → unmatched.

Two of three pairs match. The gold axiom is not rescued and contributes 0 to CQ7's TP count under this step. If CQ7 was already TP at the strict axiom step, that earlier status is preserved.

What is being measured?

Where label-matching metrics ask "do the right concepts appear?", CQCoverage asks the ontology-engineering question: "does the generated ontology, after reasoning, actually answer the competency questions it was asked to support?". By limiting closure to the subsumption hierarchy and requiring all atomic pairs to match, the metric credits ontologies that have correctly arranged the taxonomy without crediting partial or incidental overlaps.
