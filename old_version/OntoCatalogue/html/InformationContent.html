InformationContent: Semantic-level comparison using WordNet synsets and IC scores

This metric computes a semantic similarity between two labels by mapping each to its WordNet synset and comparing them with an Information-Content-based similarity (Resnik / Lin / Jiang-Conrath), returning a score in [0, 1].

Calculation Process:

  1. Map both the generated and gold labels to WordNet synsets (best-sense disambiguation).
  2. Find the lowest common ancestor (LCA) of the two synsets in the WordNet hierarchy.
  3. Compute IC(s) = -log(P(s)) for each synset using a corpus-based probability estimate.
  4. Combine via Lin similarity: 2 * IC(LCA) / (IC(s_gen) + IC(s_gold)).
  5. Take the maximum similarity across all gold labels.

Example:

Generate_Label: Beverage

Gold_Standard_Label: Wine

InformationContent(Generate_Label, Gold_Standard_Label) = 0.74

What is being measured?

This metric captures hierarchical semantic distance: pairs of concepts close to each other in the WordNet taxonomy and that share informative ancestors score higher than pairs that only share generic ancestors.
