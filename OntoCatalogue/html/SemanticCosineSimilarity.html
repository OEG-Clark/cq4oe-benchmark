SemanticCosineSimilarity: Semantic distances of dense vector representations using a pretrained encoder

This metric computes the cosine similarity between dense vector embeddings of the generated and gold labels, produced by a pretrained sentence encoder (default: an EmbeddingGemma / sentence-transformers model). The score lies in [0, 1] after rescaling.

Calculation Process:

  1. Encode both labels into fixed-dimension embedding vectors.
  2. Compute cosine similarity = (v_gen · v_gold) / (||v_gen|| · ||v_gold||).
  3. Rescale to [0, 1] if needed.
  4. Take the maximum similarity across all gold labels.
  5. A pair is considered a match if the score is greater than or equal to 0.6 (default semantic threshold).

Example:

Generate_Label: Beverage

Gold_Standard_Label: Drink

SemanticCosineSimilarity(Generate_Label, Gold_Standard_Label) = 0.79

What is being measured?

This metric captures meaning, not surface form. Synonyms and near-synonyms can score highly even when the strings have no characters in common, provided the underlying encoder learned them as semantically related during pre-training.
